# McKinsey Presentation Skill

## Governing principle
Each slide communicates one idea. Every element on the slide — headline, callouts, chart — must reinforce that idea and nothing else.

## Slide headline rules
- Action-first. State the finding or conclusion, not the topic.
  - BAD: "Security Metrics Overview"
  - GOOD: "VIP inbox attacks fell 67% across the quarter, approaching board success criterion"
- Maximum 12 words.
- Include a number whenever one is available.
- No trailing punctuation.
- No em dashes. Use a comma or period instead.

## Callout rules
Each callout has three parts:
1. **number** — the key metric, large and legible. Round to one decimal. Use "%" or "x" suffixes. Max 8 characters.
2. **label** — what the number measures. 3-5 words. Sentence case. No period.
3. **context** — the so-what. One short sentence (10-15 words). Comparative frame: vs prior period, vs benchmark, or vs success criterion.

Use at most 3 callouts per slide. Each callout must cover a distinct dimension. Never repeat the headline metric as a callout.

Color semantics:
- `success` -- metric is at or above the success criterion or benchmark p75
- `warning` -- metric is below p50 or crossing in the wrong direction
- `accent` -- neutral highlight, primary metric for the slide story
- `ink` -- secondary or context metrics

## Thesis slide rules
- `thesis_sentence`: 20-35 words. One governing argument. Names a number. Points toward action. Subject + verb + implication. No "This presentation" opener.
- `thesis_tagline`: 6-10 words. The quarter's central tension or momentum. Restrained. No exclamation marks.

## The Ask slide rules
- 3 recommendations, each with:
  - `kind`: one of POLICY, BUDGET, HEADCOUNT, EXPANSION, TRAINING, RENEWAL
  - `headline`: specific action verb + object. "Enforce MFA on T-002" not "Improve posture." Max 10 words.
  - `rationale`: one sentence naming the evidence gap and the expected outcome. 15-20 words.
- `closing_ask`: 1-2 sentences. Names the single decision the reader must make. CISO: names a budget, policy, or headcount resource. CSM: names a renewal or expansion conversation.

## Forbidden
- Passive voice
- Em dashes
- Buzzwords: leverage, robust, synergies, seamless, cutting-edge, best-in-class, world-class, game-changing
- Stacking callouts that all tell the same story
- Hedging openers ("It appears", "It seems")
- Vague recommendations ("Improve X", "Enhance Y")
